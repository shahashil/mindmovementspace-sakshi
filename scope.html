Scope of Work

Sakshi’s experience includes providing sport psychology support to athletes across a broad spectrum of age groups and skill levels, ranging from grassroots to national and international levels. Her work spans multiple sporting contexts, including cricket, football, hockey, athletics, gymnastics, snooker, tennis, swimming, badminton, and table tennis, among others.

She has worked closely with athletes within training and competitive environments, supporting areas such as confidence, performance routines, emotional regulation, and managing pressure. Her experience across diverse sporting settings allows for a contextual understanding of the unique psychological demands present within different sports and performance levels.

In addition to working with athletes, she collaborates with coaches and academies to better understand athlete behavior and performance environments. This includes contributing to the development of more structured, aligned, and supportive training systems that integrate psychological principles into everyday practice.

Her experience also extends to working with individuals from the general and rehabilitation population, applying exercise and psychological principles to support behavior change, recovery, and sustained engagement with movement.

She further engages with corporate settings through workshops and sessions focused on managing pressure, improving focus, and enhancing overall functioning in high-demand environments, drawing parallels between performance in sport and performance in professional contexts.
