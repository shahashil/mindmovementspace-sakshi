Approach

"The Mind Movement Space" Approach

Her work is grounded in understanding the relationship between mind and movement, with a focus on how thoughts, emotions, and behaviors influence the way individuals train, compete, and engage with movement. This includes developing awareness, building psychological skills, and creating structures that support consistency and adaptability over time.

Her approach is informed by evidence-based frameworks such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Rational Emotive Behavior Therapy (REBT), with an emphasis on the connection between thoughts, emotions, and behavior. These approaches are applied to help individuals build mental resilience, manage pressure, and develop more effective responses within high-performance and demanding environments.

The work is both developmental and preventative, focusing not only on addressing challenges but also on building skills that support long-term growth, performance consistency, and well-being. It extends beyond moments of difficulty to create a foundation for sustained engagement with sport and movement.

Her approach is collaborative and contextual, recognizing that each individual’s experience within sport, exercise, rehabilitation, or corporate environments is different. This includes working closely with athletes, coaches, parents or support systems, and wherever relevant to create alignment within performance environments.

The focus is on developing practical, sustainable strategies that not only enhance performance, but also support overall well-being and a more intentional relationship with movement. This may include areas such as managing pressure, emotional regulation, focus, motivation, and navigating setbacks such as injury or transition.
