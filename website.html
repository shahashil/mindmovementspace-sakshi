About

Welcome To "The Mind Movement Space"

"The Mind Movement Space" is a practice focused on supporting athletes in developing their performance through the integration of mind and movement. In sport, performance is shaped not only by physical and technical ability, but also by how individuals manage thoughts, emotions, and behaviors under pressure.

Founded and led by Sakshi Jhaveri, a Sport and Exercise Psychologist, this space works with athletes across different levels and sporting backgrounds to strengthen their mental approach to training and competition.

This work extends to coaches, individuals from the general and rehabilitation population, and corporate settings, applying similar psychological principles to support behavior change, engagement with movement, and sustainable functioning.

"Peak Performance Starts with the Right Mindset"
The philosophy guiding every session at "The Mind Movement Space".

The Approach Behind "The Mind Movement Space"

Her work is grounded in understanding the relationship between mind and movement, with a focus on how thoughts, emotions, and behaviors influence the way individuals train, compete, and engage with movement.

The approach is structured, collaborative, and tailored to the individual, drawing on evidence-based frameworks such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Rational Emotive Behavior Therapy (REBT). It focuses on developing awareness, building mental skills, and creating practical strategies that support consistency, adaptability, and performance under pressure.

The Journey Behind "The Mind Movement Space"

Sakshi Jhaveri's journey into Sport and Exercise Psychology is shaped by her own experiences as a competitive badminton player. Through sport, she was exposed to the demands of performance, expectations, consistency, and the challenges of navigating setbacks, while also experiencing how movement can serve as a space for structure, focus, and stress management.

These experiences shaped her understanding of the connection between mind and movement, which continues to guide her work today, supporting athletes in not only improving performance, but also developing greater awareness of how they think, feel, and respond within their sporting journeys.
